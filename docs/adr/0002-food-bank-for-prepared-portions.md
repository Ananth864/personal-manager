# Food Bank tracks prepared portions separately from Ingredient Inventory

Cooking a Recipe or Ad-hoc Recipe for a Meal Slot prepares that slot's meal — the slot eats one of the portions — and banks the remaining `servings − 1` portions (clamped at zero) into a separate ledger called the Food Bank. A Meal Slot is filled either by a fresh Cook (consumes ingredients, banks the remaining portions) or by withdrawing an existing portion from the Food Bank (no ingredient effect). Raw ingredients and prepared food are never mixed into one inventory.

Availability is derived, not stored: `produced` (from past Cooks) + `planned` (projected from uncooked recipe/ad-hoc slots in the plannable horizon — current and next Week) − `reserved` (Food Bank withdrawal slots). The `planned` term lets a plan reserve the future portions of a meal cooked earlier in the week (e.g. "cook Chili Monday, eat it Tue–Thu"); when that Cook fires, its portions move from `planned` to `produced` with no double-count.

Rejected alternatives: (a) no Food Bank — every meal cooks fresh, which double-decrements ingredients for batch cooking and makes the shopping list lie when one cook is meant to feed multiple slots; rejected because the user batch-cooks. (b) model prepared portions as pseudo-ingredients in the main Inventory — rejected because it conflates raw and prepared food, breaks "Cook is the only thing that mutates Tracked Inventory," and makes the shopping-list derivation ambiguous about whether a quantity is raw or cooked. (c) bank all `servings` on Cook (the slot eats none) — rejected because it over-produces (a 2-serving cook would yield 2 banked + the slot's meal = 3 meals from 2 servings). (d) Food Bank drawn only from past Cooks (no `planned` term) — rejected because it blocks planning multi-meal cooks; the agent could not auto-plan "cook once, eat the remaining portions across the week."
